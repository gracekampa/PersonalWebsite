about me

I'm kind of obsessed with making sure user interfaces are actually usable. If you have written a program that can route you to the Holy Grail, but no one knows how to use it, it's kind of useless, right? That's why I love the marriage of coding and visuals-- making a beautiful system that people can use.

MedTracker Prototype

MedTracker was a group project I worked on for my Human-Computer Interaction class. It was essentially a medicine tracking app that gave the user reminders to take their medication. The end project was just a high-fidelity prototype, but throughout the semester we worked on wireframing, usability testing, low-fidelity prototyping, and continually gauging feedback to improve the prototype each iteration.
